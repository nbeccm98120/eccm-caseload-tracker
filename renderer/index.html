Getting Started

When you first open the app you will go through a simple 3-step setup:

  • Step 1 — Your Info: Enter your full name and your coach or supervisor name. Both are required. Your name is permanent and used to identify your backup files — it cannot be changed after setup.
  • Step 2 — Data Folder: Choose where your records are automatically saved. Select your backup folder — ideally on OneDrive or a shared drive so your data is always protected. This step is required.
  • Step 3 — Confirm: Review your name, coach, and folder — then click Get Started.

Once set up, you will land on the Dashboard. Go to Caseload → + Add Consumer to begin adding your consumers.

💡 Setting up on a new computer? Click Restore from Backup on the setup screen instead of entering your info again. Pick your backup file and all your data will be restored automatically.

Your coach name can be updated anytime via Actions → Details.

Data & Records

How your data is saved: Every change is saved automatically to your computer. You never need to click Save for individual entries.

Auto-sync: Every 7 minutes (if changes were made) and every time you close the app, your records are automatically copied to your backup folder. When closing you will briefly see a "Saving your work..." overlay — this is normal.

📤 Save / Share Records
Opens a consumer picker. Select all consumers for a full backup, or specific consumers for a case transfer. The file is named automatically — click Rename to give it a custom name. Find it under Actions → Save / Share Records.
📥 Load Records
Loads records from a file. Pick which consumers to import — choose Add to My Caseload for new consumers or Update Existing for consumers already in your caseload. A Preview button shows exactly what would change before you confirm. Records are saved to your backup folder immediately after loading. Find it under Actions → Load Records.
💾 Save Now
Immediately saves your records to your backup folder without closing the app. Find it under Actions → Save Now.
🔄 Restore a Previous Backup
Shows all backup files in your backup folder. Click Restore next to any file to load it. Records are saved immediately after restoring. Find it under Actions → Restore a Previous Backup.
⚠ Set Data Folder: Only change this if you are moving your backup folder or setting up a new save location. Changing it incorrectly means your old folder will stop being updated. Contact your coach before changing this setting.
Managing Consumers

Go to the Caseload tab to manage your consumers. Click + Add Consumer to add a new consumer.

Required fields: MCI#, First Name, Last Name, and Waiver Type. All four must be filled in to save.

MCI# is always 9 digits — shorter numbers are padded automatically (e.g. 32418 → 000032418).

Status chips: Use Active for current consumers. Use Inactive for consumers who have closed services, transferred to another SCO, or moved to a different SC within ECCM. Inactive consumers are hidden from all tracker views but remain in your records for history.

Comments / Notes: Click the 💬 button on any consumer row to view or add notes. Notes are visible in the caseload list and can be edited at any time.

Deleting a consumer: Only do this if a consumer was added by mistake. If a consumer closed or transferred, use Inactive status instead. Deletion permanently removes ALL their monitoring, ISP, PUNS, Emergency, and A/B history and cannot be undone.

Filtering and sorting: Each table has a small ▾ arrow on the Waiver and Status column headers. Click it to filter by that column or sort the list. When a filter is active a ⊘ Filtered ✕ badge appears next to the search bar — click it to clear all filters. Use the search bar to find consumers by name or MCI# — an ✕ button appears to clear the search quickly.

Case Transfers

When a consumer permanently transfers from one SC to another, you can export and import their full record including all monitoring, ISP, PUNS, Emergency, and A/B history.

Transferring a consumer out (your caseload → another SC):

  • Go to Actions → Save / Share Records
  • Select only the consumer(s) being transferred
  • Give the file a descriptive name (e.g. Smith_James_Transfer_May2026)
  • Save and share the file with the receiving SC
  • Set the transferred consumer to Inactive in your caseload

Receiving a transferred consumer (another SC → your caseload):

  • Go to Actions → Load Records
  • Pick the transfer file — a consumer picker shows all consumers in the file
  • Select the consumers you want to add and choose Add to My Caseload
  • Their complete history (monitoring, ISP, PUNS, etc.) imports into your caseload

Merging updated records — if you already have a consumer and receive updated data from another SC:

  • Import the file — consumers you already have will show highlighted in red as conflicts
  • Click Preview on any conflict consumer to see exactly what would change
  • Choose Update Existing to update with incoming data, or Skip to leave unchanged
  • Merge updates all consumer fields (name, waiver, DOB) plus all tracker data
Monitoring

Go to Caseload Tracker → Monitoring. Use the month strip to navigate, or click This Month to return to the current month.

Logging a contact: Click Log on any consumer row. Fill in Contact Type, Contact Date, and Tool Completed (HCSIS) date. All three must be filled in for the entry to count as Done. The contact date must fall within the selected month and year.

Status indicators:

  • Done This Month — all three fields complete ✓
  • Pending Tool Date — contact type and date entered, tool date missing
  • Pending Contact Date — contact type entered, date missing
  • Pending Contact Type — contact date entered, type missing
  • Due This Month — monitoring is due this month based on last contact
  • Critical — Past Due — monitoring was due and is now overdue
  • Missed — a past month where no complete entry was logged
  • No Monitoring Logged Yet — new consumer with no history
  • Not Applicable — Private ICF/MR or Other waiver

Done consumers are hidden from the main view — the list shows only consumers needing attention. Click the ✓ Done chip to see completed entries.

New consumers / backfilling: For consumers with no monitoring history, the Log modal shows month and year dropdowns so you can enter a previous contact. Once the first entry is saved, normal date validation applies going forward.

Monitoring frequencies:

  • Consolidated Waiver / Community Living Waiver — every 2 months
  • P/FDS Waiver — every 3 months
  • Base / SC Services Only — every 12 months
  • Private ICF/MR / Other — Not Applicable

Contact Types: H (Home), HV (Home Video), D (Day Program), DV (Day Video), C (Community), CV (Community Video)

Filtering: Click the ▾ arrow on the Waiver column to filter by waiver type. The filter respects the active chip — on the Done chip, only waivers of Done consumers appear. A ⊘ Filtered ✕ badge shows when a filter is active.

ISP Timeline

Go to Caseload Tracker → ISP. Click Edit on any consumer. Start with the Annual Review Date — all other deadlines calculate automatically.

Auto-calculated deadlines:

  • Start Date — 120 days before Annual Review
  • Last ISP Date — 60 days before Annual Review
  • Submit By — 50 days before Annual Review
  • Invite Due — 30 days before scheduled ISP meeting
  • Future ARUD — exactly 1 year after Annual Review (leap year safe)

Submitting an ISP: Once you enter a Submitted date, all deadline columns grey out with a strikethrough and a → Next ARUD button appears on the row. When the ISP is approved and the new annual date is confirmed, click → Next ARUD to advance the timeline to the next cycle and clear the submitted date.

ISP Status is set manually: In Progress → Pending Approval → Approved → Needs Revision. The app does not change status automatically.

Dates shown in red are past due. Dates in yellow/orange are due within 14 days. Greyed strikethrough columns mean the ISP has been submitted.

PUNS

Go to Caseload Tracker → PUNS. Click Edit to enter PUNS information for each consumer.

Typical workflow:

  • SC completes PUNS with consumer → enters Submitted to Coach date
  • Coach reviews and finalizes in HCSIS → SC enters the Finalized Date
  • Entering a new Finalized Date automatically clears the Submitted to Coach date

PUNS Due Date is calculated automatically — 334 days after the Finalized Date.

Categories: Planning, Critical, Emergency, Child, All Needs Met. Child and All Needs Met categories are automatically set to Inactive with no due date calculated.

Emergency / FFA / Voter

Go to Caseload Tracker → Emergency / FFA / Voter. Click Edit to enter completion dates for each consumer.

Emergency Plan, FFA (Free from Abuse), and Voter Registration all renew annually. The due date is automatically calculated as 365 days after the completion date. Each of the three renews independently.

Voter Registration — 18+ only: Voter Registration only applies to consumers who are 18 or older. For consumers under 18, the Voter Done field is greyed out and cannot be edited. The Voter columns in the table will also be blank for these consumers. Once a consumer turns 18 (based on their Date of Birth on file), the field becomes available automatically.

Dates shown in red are overdue. Dates in yellow/orange are due within 30 days. The actual due date is shown below each status pill.

SCO P4P (A/B Tracker)

The SCO Pay for Performance Metric tracker starts May 2026. Go to the A/B Tracker tab and select the month. Click Log to enter codes for each consumer.

Status rules:

  • Met — 1 or more A codes, OR 2 or more B codes total (same code counts multiple times)
  • In Progress — exactly 1 B code logged in the current or a future month
  • Not Started — nothing logged yet for the current or a future month
  • Missed — a past month where the requirement was not met
  • Not Applicable — Private ICF/MR or Other waiver

When the same B code is entered more than once it displays as B4 ×2 in the table. Months before May 2026 are not tracked.

Dashboard & Alerts

The Dashboard shows everything needing attention, grouped by category: Monitoring, PUNS, ISP, and Emergency.

Click Log on any alert row to open the logging modal directly. Once the requirement is met, the alert disappears automatically.

Click ✕ to temporarily dismiss an alert. Use Restore All to bring dismissed alerts back.

Use the search box at the top to narrow down alerts by consumer name or MCI#. A ▾ arrow on the Waiver column lets you filter alerts by waiver type. When a filter is active a ⊘ Filtered ✕ badge appears — click it to clear.

Alert labels show the specific type — Emergency Preparedness Critical, FFA (Free from Abuse) Due Soon, Monitoring Due (every 2mo) — so you always know exactly what is needed.
